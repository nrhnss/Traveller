Interesting Places

Jeju Island

Jeju Island, known as the "Island of the Gods," is a stunning volcanic island off the southern coast of South Korea. It’s famous for its breathtaking landscapes, unique volcanic formations, pristine beaches, and lush greenery. Hallasan Mountain, a dormant volcano, stands majestically at its center, offering hiking trails with panoramic views and diverse flora and fauna. Jeju also features scenic waterfalls like Cheonjiyeon and Jeongbang, picturesque lava tubes such as Manjanggul, and the Seongsan Ilchulbong "Sunrise Peak," a UNESCO World Heritage Site that offers spectacular sunrise views.

Gyeongbokgung Palace

Gyeongbokgung Palace, located in the heart of Seoul, is the largest and most iconic of South Korea's Five Grand Palaces. Built in 1395 during the Joseon Dynasty, it served as the royal residence and government center. Gyeongbokgung is a masterpiece of traditional Korean architecture, featuring grand halls, elaborate gates, and scenic gardens with lotus ponds and pagodas. The main throne hall, Geunjeongjeon, stands out for its intricate detailing and was used for important ceremonies and royal gatherings.

Nami Island

Nami Island, located near Seoul in South Korea, is a picturesque, half-moon-shaped island famous for its stunning natural beauty and tranquil ambiance. Known for its tree-lined paths, especially the iconic Metasequoia Lane, Nami Island showcases vibrant seasonal landscapes—from cherry blossoms in spring and lush green foliage in summer to colorful autumn leaves and a snowy winter wonderland.

Songwol-dong Fairy Tale Village

Songwol-dong Fairy Tale Village, located in Incheon, South Korea, is a whimsical neighborhood transformed into a colorful wonderland inspired by beloved fairy tales and childhood stories. Originally a residential area, it was revitalized by artists and local initiatives, turning walls, streets, and buildings into vibrant murals and sculptures.
